Map-description


Above we have the datamap on the left and a detailed view for it on the right. The map shows the international ranking of the countries according to its top university rankings. Thus it has a chloropleth with green color range to indicate the ranking of the best countries in terms of its top university rankings in the world. The darker the shade of the country the higher the rank of its top ranked institution worldwide. Also note that since the dataset does not contain information for all the countries most of the countries have been shown in black stating that information regarding their ranking cannot be given. By clicking on a particular country you can view the spending of the selected country's goverment on education(both public and private institution) as a percentage of the nation's total GDP. The bar-chart on the right shows bars for years 1995, 2000, 2005, 2009, 2010, 2011. The higher the bar height the more the percentage of total GDP spent on education.

Insight

By looking at Norway we see a good and gradual increase of goverment spending from 5.7% in 2005 to a remarkable 7.3% in 2011. Norway's percentage of money spent on education is quite higher than many nations and when compared to its neighbouring country Russia, we see that Russia's spending highest in 2009 has been gradually declining and is at 3.9% just a shy more than half of norway. But an important thing to remember is that the spending is a percentage of the total GDP of the country hence a country with 10 times the GDP of another will still have spent more with half the percentage of spending on GDP.
